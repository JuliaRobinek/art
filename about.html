about

Julia Robinek is a painter and meditation guide whose practice explores the relationship between inner belief systems and the external worlds they shape. Her work examines how ideas about self, nature, and value influence the ways we design our lives, our societies, and our environments.

Working primarily with acrylic on canvas, she creates figurative compositions through layered paint. Strong, often charged colors generate images that balance intimacy and tension, addressing vulnerability and resilience in both human and natural forms.

Her paintings focus on two interconnected concerns: the consequences of human design, including ecological and psychological destruction, and the inner patterns of perception and belief that give rise to these outcomes. By tracing how mental and cultural frameworks become embedded in social and environmental structures, her work invites reflection on responsibility, empathy, and the possibility of reconnection with the living world.

Alongside her artistic practice, Julia Robinek has studied value sensitive design in technology and economics, informing her understanding of how values shape material and immaterial systems. She holds advanced degrees in IT Communication, Web Design, and Economics, has course-based training in painting, and is a certified Meditation Teacher through the University of California, Berkeley’s Greater Good Science Center.

Her interdisciplinary background allows her painting practice to operate as both critical inquiry and contemplative experience.
